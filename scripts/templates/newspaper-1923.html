THREE VANISH WITHOUT TRACE
IN MILLBROOK WOODS
Search Parties Comb Forest for Missing Residents; No Clues Found After Week-Long Effort

MILLBROOK â€” The disappearance of three local residents has left this quiet community in a state of bewilderment and fear. After seven days of intensive searching, authorities have found no trace of Harold Finch, 34, Margaret Owens, 28, and young Peter Ashworth, 19.

The three were last seen on the evening of October 19th, each departing for an evening constitutional in the woods north of Hartwell Road. None returned to their homes that night.

Millbrook Woods â€” Site of Disappearances
The dense forest north of town where three residents vanished.

Sheriff Thomas Holbrook expressed frustration at the lack of evidence. "In all my years, I've never seen anything like it," he told this reporter. "No tracks, no belongings, no sign of struggle. It's as if the earth itself swallowed them whole."

Local residents have begun to whisper of an old legend â€” that the woods are home to something unnatural. Mrs. Edith Crane, who lives nearest the forest, claims to have heard "unearthly sounds" on the night of the disappearances.

"It was like nothing I've ever heard," Mrs. Crane stated, visibly shaken. "A low humming, and then... voices. But not human voices. Not quite."

Church attendance has doubled since the disappearances, with Reverend Morris offering special prayers for the missing and their families.

Some older residents recall similar incidents from decades past, though official records are scarce. Former town clerk William Marsh, 78, claims his grandfather spoke of disappearances in those same woods dating back to the town's founding.

"Every fifty years or so," Marsh said, shaking his head. "That's what my grandfather told me. Every fifty years, the woods take their due."

Sheriff Holbrook has requested assistance from state authorities. The search continues.
