THE THRESHOLD SOCIETY
Charter of Foundation
Established in the Year of Our Lord, 1823
✦

WE, the undersigned, being of sound mind and solemn purpose, do hereby establish this Society for the sacred duty of GUARDING THE BOUNDARIES BETWEEN WORLDS. Having witnessed that which cannot be explained by natural philosophy, we bind ourselves to eternal vigilance.

The land upon which the township of Millbrook now stands has been known to us as a place of THRESHOLD — where the veil between our world and that which lies beyond grows thin. It is our charge to ensure that what sleeps beneath remains undisturbed.

— ARTICLES OF SACRED DUTY —

ARTICLE I. Members shall maintain vigilance over the designated forest lands, particularly during periods of celestial significance.

ARTICLE II. The rituals of containment, as recorded in the Black Ledger, shall be performed without fail upon each half-century, lest the Boundary weaken.

ARTICLE III. No member shall speak of Society matters to those uninitiated, upon pain of excommunication and that which follows.

"We stand at the Threshold.
We guard the Gate.
What was bound shall remain bound.
Until the final darkness takes us all."
Ezekiel Ward, Founder
Josiah Crane, Secretary
Nathaniel Marsh, Keeper
