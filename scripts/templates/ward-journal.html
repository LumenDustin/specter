October 26th, 1923
Something is terribly wrong in Millbrook. I write this by candlelight, for the electric has gone out again - the third time this week. Father says it is merely the autumn storms, but I know better.
I saw them again tonight - the members of the Society, walking in procession toward the woods. Uncle Ezekiel led them, carrying that dreadful black book he guards so jealously. Mother forbade me from following, but I watched from my window until the lanterns disappeared into the trees.
They have been preparing for weeks. Whispered conversations that cease when I enter the room. Strange symbols drawn in chalk, quickly erased. And always, always, that low humming that seems to come from everywhere and nowhere.
Harold Finch came to the house this afternoon. He looked terrified - said he had been chosen for the "great honor." When I asked what honor, Uncle Ezekiel appeared and sent me away. But I heard Harold weeping through the door.
What are they planning? What happens tomorrow night?
The humming grows louder. I can feel it in my teeth, in my bones. Something is coming. Something is waking up.
God help us all.
