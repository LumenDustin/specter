NARRATIVE

On the evening of October 31, 1973 (Halloween), three residents of Millbrook were reported missing after failing to return from separate walks near the woods north of Hartwell Road. An extensive search was conducted over the following 72 hours involving MPD personnel, state police, and approximately 200 civilian volunteers.

No physical evidence was recovered. No clothing, personal effects, or signs of struggle were found at or near the suspected location. K-9 units lost all scent trails at the same coordinates (approximately 200 yards into the tree line).

Multiple witnesses reported unusual phenomena on the night in question, including: unexplained lights in the forest, a persistent low-frequency humming sound, and sudden temperature drops. These accounts remain unverified.

Note: This is the SECOND mass disappearance in these woods. First occurred October 1923 ‚Äî exactly 50 years prior. Three victims then as well. Same circumstances. Files from 1923 incident are missing from archives. ‚Äî W.T.

Investigation is ongoing. All leads have been exhausted. Families have been notified. Case remains open pending new information.

INVESTIGATING OFFICER ASSESSMENT

After 30 years in law enforcement, I have never encountered a case like this. The complete absence of evidence, combined with the historical precedent, suggests factors beyond conventional explanation. I am requesting that this file be flagged for special handling and that access be restricted to senior personnel only.
